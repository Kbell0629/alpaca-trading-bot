## Trading Involves Risk of Loss

**Trading stocks, options, and other securities involves substantial risk of loss and is not suitable for every investor.** You could lose some or all of your invested capital. Past performance — including any backtest results, paper trading results, hypothetical returns, or historical profits shown in the Service — is **not indicative of future results**.

Specific risks include but are not limited to:

- **Market risk** — Individual securities and the broader market can decline in value for reasons that cannot be predicted.
- **Volatility risk** — Stock prices, especially for leveraged ETFs (e.g. SOXL, TQQQ), can move 5-20% or more in a single day.
- **Gap risk** — Stops cannot protect against overnight gaps below the stop price. A stock may open at a price below your stop and execute at a much lower price than expected.
- **Liquidity risk** — Thin markets may prevent orders from filling at desired prices.
- **Options risk** — Options strategies, including cash-secured puts and covered calls (the "Wheel" strategy), can result in assignment of shares at prices significantly above current market value. Put sellers can be forced to buy shares during market crashes.

- **Regulatory risk** — Government policy changes, SEC rule changes, or broker policy changes can affect your ability to trade or the cost of trading.
- **Total loss** — It is possible to lose 100% of the capital in an account, and in some cases (e.g. naked options, margin) more than 100%.

## Paper Trading vs. Live Trading

The Service supports both **paper trading** (simulated, no real money) and **live trading** (real money). Paper trading does not accurately reflect live trading conditions because:

- No real execution queue; orders fill instantly at assumed prices
- No broker queue priority or routing decisions
- No emotional pressure
- No real market impact from your orders
- Market data in paper mode may be delayed or differ from live feeds

**Paper trading success does not imply live trading success.** Before enabling live trading, consider starting with a small amount of capital you are fully prepared to lose.

## Pattern Day Trader Rule

U.S. margin accounts with under $25,000 are subject to the Pattern Day Trader rule (FINRA 4210). Placing more than 3 day trades in 5 business days may result in your account being classified as a PDT account and restricted from further day trading. **The Service does not monitor or enforce PDT compliance for your account.** You are responsible for tracking your own day trades.
